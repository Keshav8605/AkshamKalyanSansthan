Women's Program

When a Woman Rises, the Whole Family Rises

Our women empowerment program equips women with vocational skills, entrepreneurship training, and the confidence to become financially independent breadwinners.

About This Program

From Dependence to Self-Reliance

Millions of Indian women, especially in rural areas, lack the skills and resources to earn independently. Our program bridges this gap through practical, market-relevant vocational training.

We run community-based training centers where women learn tailoring, handicrafts, beauty services, food processing, and digital literacy. Beyond skills, we provide micro-enterprise guidance, connect graduates with markets, and build self-help groups (SHGs) for sustained economic support.

Our Process

How We Empower Women

01

Community Outreach

Our female coordinators visit communities, identify women interested in skill development, and conduct enrollment drives. We specifically target widows, single mothers, and women from BPL families.

02

Skill Training Programs

3-to-6-month intensive training programs in tailoring, embroidery, beauty services, food processing, and basic computer skills. Each batch of 20-25 women learns from certified trainers.

03

Entrepreneurship & Market Linkage

Graduates receive guidance on starting home-based enterprises, pricing products, managing finances, and connecting with local markets, exhibitions, and online platforms.

04

Self-Help Group Formation

Trained women are organized into Self-Help Groups (SHGs) that pool savings, access micro-credit, and support each other's enterprises — creating a sustainable support ecosystem.

Why This Matters

Educate a Woman, Empower a Nation

When women earn, they invest 90% back into their families — compared to 35% for men. Women's economic empowerment creates a multiplier effect.

  • Family Transformation

    An earning mother means better nutrition, education, and healthcare for the entire family. The ripple effect reaches generations.

  • Gender Equality

    Financial independence gives women a voice in household decisions, reduces domestic violence, and builds a more equitable society.

  • Economic Growth

    Every woman who starts a micro-enterprise creates 2-3 additional jobs in her community, driving rural economic development.
