Hygiene Program

Breaking the Silence on Menstrual Health

Periods shouldn't stop progress. Our program ensures every girl has access to sanitary products and the knowledge to manage her menstrual health with dignity.

About This Program

Dignity, Health & Confidence for Every Girl

In India, 23 million girls drop out of school annually due to lack of menstrual hygiene facilities. Our program addresses this silent crisis head-on.

We distribute sanitary napkin kits to teenage girls in government schools, conduct menstrual health awareness sessions led by trained female volunteers, and install sanitary napkin vending machines in partnered schools. Our approach is gentle, respectful, and focused on normalizing conversations around periods.

Our Approach

A Respectful, Comprehensive Process

01

School Partnership

We partner with government and aided schools, meeting with principals and female teachers to understand the specific needs of their girl students.

02

Awareness Workshops

Trained female volunteers conduct age-appropriate sessions on menstrual health, hygiene practices, and myth-busting in comfortable, girls-only settings.

03

Kit Distribution

Each girl receives a discreet hygiene kit containing biodegradable sanitary napkins for 6 months, along with a cloth pouch and hygiene guide booklet.

04

Infrastructure Support

We install sanitary napkin vending machines and disposal units in school washrooms, creating a sustainable long-term solution beyond our visits.

Why This Matters

Periods Are Natural. Shame Shouldn't Be.

Menstrual hygiene isn't just a health issue — it's an education issue, a gender equality issue, and a human dignity issue.

  • Keeping Girls in School

    Girls who manage periods safely are 5x less likely to drop out. Every kit we give is an investment in a girl's future.

  • Health & Safety

    Unsafe menstrual practices lead to infections. Our biodegradable products are safe, comfortable, and environmentally responsible.

  • Breaking the Stigma

    Our workshops normalize period conversations, empowering girls to speak openly and seek help without shame or embarrassment.
