About This Program

Greening India, One Sapling at a Time

India loses approximately 1.5 million hectares of forest cover annually. Our tree plantation drives mobilize communities to restore green cover in barren lands, riverbanks, school grounds, and public spaces.

Each plantation drive involves community participation — from school children to senior citizens. We don't just plant; we nurture. Every sapling is geo-tagged, assigned a caretaker, and monitored for 3 years to ensure a survival rate above 80%.

Our Process

From Seed to Sustainable Forest

01

Site Selection & Species Planning

Our team surveys barren lands, highway patches, school compounds, and riverbanks. We select native, drought-resistant species suited to the local soil and climate conditions.

02

Community Mobilization

We partner with schools, colleges, corporate volunteers, and community groups to build large planting teams. Each participant is trained on proper planting, pit preparation, and initial watering.

03

Plantation Day

Organized mass plantation events with 500–2,000 participants planting saplings simultaneously. Each sapling is geo-tagged and assigned to a local caretaker from the community.

04

3-Year Nurturing Program

Post-planting, local caretakers water and protect saplings for 3 years. Our team conducts quarterly audits, replaces dead saplings, and tracks the overall survival rate with photo documentation.

Why This Matters

The Planet Can't Wait. Neither Can We.

Climate change is no longer a future threat — it's today's reality. Trees are the most cost-effective solution.

  • Carbon Sequestration

    A single tree absorbs 22 kg of CO₂ per year. Our 15,000 trees remove over 330 tonnes of carbon annually from the atmosphere.

  • Water Conservation

    Trees prevent soil erosion, recharge groundwater, and regulate the water cycle — critical for drought-prone regions.

  • Biodiversity Protection

    Native trees create habitats for birds, insects, and wildlife — restoring ecosystems that urbanization has destroyed.
